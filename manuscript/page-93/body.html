tyaktva raga dveshau vyudasya cha (18/51)

Translation:

vishuddha (fully purified) buddhya (with the intelligence) yukto (engaged) dhritya (with determination and patience) atmanam (the self) niyamya (regulating) cha (also) shabdadin (such as sound) vishayan (sense objects) tyaktva (giving up) ragadveshau (attachment and hatred) cha (also) vyudasya (laying aside) Being purified by his intelligence controlling the mind with determination, giving up the objects of sense gratification, he also remains free from attachment and hatred.

Spiritual Commentary:

Having attained the tranquil state at the after effect poise of kriya and holding on to the Ultimate Self, the kriyaban remains absorbed by means of concentration, meditation and samadhi ignoring sounds, desires and violence which naturally go away at the after effect
